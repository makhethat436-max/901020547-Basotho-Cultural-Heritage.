TRADITIONAL MUSIC AND DANCE

BASOTHO TRADITIONAL MUSIC

Basotho traditional music features a variety of indigenous instruments such as "lesiba", drums, and other percussion instruments.

BASOTHO TRADITIONAL DANCING

Basotho traditional dance is characterized by energetic and dynamic movements, often performed to the rythm of drums and other instruments.

BASOTHO TRADITONAL DANCES AND INSTRUMENTS

Mokorotlo

A dance that mimics daily activities, often performed in agricultural contexts, symbolizing hard work and importance of agriculture in Basotho life

Mohobole

A lively dance showcasing energetic movements, often performed during celebrations and special occasions, embodying joy and unity among Basotho people.

Mokhibo

A traditional dance performed by wowen, chracterized by gentle movements and gestures, often telling stories of Basotho history and culture.

Khati

It is a traditional dance performed by young women as to show their energy and strength.

Lesokoana

A dance with cultural significance of praying of rain. It is often played by women

Ho khoasa litali

It is often performed in specific contexts. Young boys are the ones who typically perform it.

Ndlamo

This is Basotho's traditional dance that takes place during cultural events and celerations. It is characterized by rythms and energetic movements

Selia-lia

A dance performed by marriage-age individuals with the purpose of selecting the right patner

Litolobonya

Gener of music and dance perforemed exclusively by women and girls, often invoving singing with poetic lyrics.

Maqekha

A special dance that is part of the first girls' initiation, accompanied by instruments like morupa and restricted to initiations.

Serobolela

Sesotho songs particularly sung by girls on summer nights and later influencing the Famo dance.

Lipina tsa mokopu

A dance performed by girls while wearing white garments and bear feet.

Thomo

This is a stringed instument used in traditional music and storytelling. It is typically used by women.

Khabulu-bulu

It is a traditional instrument made of a rhino horn.

Sekebuku

A wooden jaw harp played by women and girls.

Lekolulo

A type of flute played by herding boys.

Morupa

A small drum made of clay pot and animal skin. It is used in women initiation.

Mamokhorong

A bowed monochord instrument made a tin can resonator, played with a friction bow.

Lekoko

Roll of hardened cow skin beaten with sticks as to produce dull thumping sound.

Meruthoane

Ankle rattles worn by dancers to add percussion to musical performance.

Mokhope

A bow and wind instrument played by women, where elephant grass is frictioned onto a wire and resonated by the mouth.

Lenaka le Lekhitlane

Spritual cow or goat horns used by traditional doctors and medium.

Koriana

Koriana refers to an accordion-like instrument used in Famo music.
